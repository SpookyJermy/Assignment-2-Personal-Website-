Attabad Lake

Attabad Lake is a lake located in the Gojal region of Hunza Valley in Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan. It was created in January 2010 as a result of a major landslide in Attabad that blocked the flow of the Hunza River and flooded several villages1. The lake has become one of the main tourist attractions in Gilgit-Baltistan, offering activities such as boating, jet-skiing, fishing and other recreational activities. Attabad Lake is about 21 km (13 mi) long and has a depth of more than 100 m (330 ft), making it one of the highest lakes in the world1. The lake has a stunning crystal blue color that contrasts with the green meadows and snow-capped mountains around it. The lake also submerged a part of the Karakoram Highway, which had to be rebuilt with a tunnel through the landslide dam. Attabad Lake was formed by a natural disaster that killed 20 people and displaced thousands more. The lake also submerged some ancient religious monuments, such as Buddhist stupas, temples and mosques2. The lake is a reminder of the power and beauty of nature, as well as the resilience and adaptability of the local people2.
